## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are university students managing notes, course resources, and profile verification during busy semester workflows. Secondary users are admins who review verification and maintain academic data. The core job is to capture study knowledge quickly, retrieve the right material fast, and complete required academic steps without friction.

### Brand Personality
**Focused, practical, trustworthy.**  
The interface should feel like a real student workspace (not a marketing shell): calm, direct, and utility-first. AI should feel supportive and subtle rather than flashy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a clean academic workspace aesthetic with restrained depth, clear hierarchy, and deliberate spacing. Preserve both light and dark themes with system support, and keep the existing token foundation (neural purple + warm neutrals + semantic accents). Favor crisp components, readable typography, and low-noise surfaces over decorative effects.

### Design Principles
1. **Student-task first**: Prioritize flows for writing notes, finding resources, and completing verification.
2. **Clarity over novelty**: Keep labels, layouts, and actions immediately understandable.
3. **Consistent system usage**: Reuse existing tokens, component variants, and spacing rhythm before inventing new patterns.
4. **Signal with restraint**: Use brand color and motion to guide attention, not decorate.
5. **Accessible by default**: Maintain contrast, keyboard support, and reduced-motion-safe interactions.
